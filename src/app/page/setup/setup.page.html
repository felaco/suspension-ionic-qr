Rear Suspension Setup

LME-SWP-00025 - page 1369

NOTICE The minimum weight of each filled rear suspension is 895kg. Use an approved lift device that can safely lift and support the component weight WARNING Failure to complete each step can result in suspension failure. Complete each step shown in this procedure
  • Follow the haul truck safety precautions [Reference: Safety, page 45]
  • The rear suspension shall be checked, adjusted, and charged as a pair
  • Fully discharge the nitrogen from the rear suspensions
 {{ tool.name }}

Step 1

Lift and support th rear end of the chassis until the chrome length X_0 is reached on eacg rear suspension. Use the Suspension Assessment charts to find the X_0 dimension.

Show NOTICE The weight of the rear axle must remain on the ground

Step 2

Remove the suspension transducer cover from the rear suspensions.

Step 3

Remove the rod protection cover from the rear suspension.

CAUTION
  • Damage to the piston rod can occur when the protective cover is removed
  • Be careful not to damage the piston surface while working on the suspension with the protective cover removed

Using a hose from the chargin kit, connect one end to the suspension valve and the opposite end into a waste oil container. (Repeat this step for the opposite rear suspension).

Step 1

Open the gas valve and slowly lower the chassis until chrome length X_oil is reached on each rear suspension. Excess oil will flow through the hoses and into the waste oil container. Use the Suspension Assessment Charts to find the X_oil dimension

Show CAUTION
  • Trapped air will affect the oil volume and may result in suspension failure
  • Make sure to release trapped air from inside the suspension

Step 2

Make sure that oil flows from both suspensions. Keep the cassis in the position until flow of oil or oil foam slows down.

NOTICE If oil does not flow from both the suspensions:
  • The oil level is low and more oil must be added
See here how

Step 3

Close the gas valves

Step 4

Slowly the chassis until the weight of the haul truck is fully supported by the suspensions and verify chrome length X ≥ X_oil - 5 mm (0.2 in).

If X ≥ X_oil + 5 mm (0.2 in) on either suspension (when supporting the weight of the empty haul truck) procced to Suspension Oil Fill See here how

If X ≤ X_oil + 5 mm (0.2 in) on both suspensions (when supporting the weight of the empty haul truck) procced to the next step

Step 5

Lift the rear end of the chassis until the suspension is fully extended

NOTICE The weight of the rear axle must remain on the ground

Step 6

Remove the ends of the hoses from the waste oil container and connect each of them to the nitrogen chargin kit manifold

NOTICE

it is best to dedicate hoses for either fluid use r gas charging. If the same hose must be used for both steps, ensure any residual oil is drained from inside the hose before continuing.

Step 7

Set the nitrogen charing kit regulator to approximately 5 bar (72 psi) and open the gas valves on both suspensions

The following is not a separate procedure, it is done in combination with the suspension setup

Step 1

Lift and support the rear end of the chassis until the chrome length X_0 is reached in each rear suspension.

Wait until both suspensions are stable at chrome length X_0 before proceeding to the next step

NOTICE

The weight of the rear axle must remain on the ground

Step 2

Adjust the gas charge to the specified precharge pressure P_0 (reference the Suspension Assesment Chart for this pressure)

See here

Step 3

Verify the P_0 value stabilizes on both rear suspensions

Step 4

Close the gas charging valves on both suspensions

Step 5

Use the touch screen in the operator cab to verify the rear suspension pressures read within ± 1.5 bar (21 psi) of P_0.

If the pressures are not within tolerance troubleshooting is required

NOTICE

The pre-charge pressure and the pressure reading on the touch screen in the operator crab must be within ± 1.5 bar (21 psi)

Incorrect pre-charge pressure can cause suspension failure

Step 6

Disconnect both the hoses from the manifold of the nitrogen charging kit, and from each of the rear suspension gas valves. Replace the gas valve protective caps.

Step 7

Reinstall the rod protection covers and the transducer covers.

Step 8

Procedure complete. Lower the haul truck to the ground
