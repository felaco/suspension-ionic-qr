Rear Suspension Oil Fill and Pressure Testing Procedure

  • This procedure gives the instructions to pressure test and to fill the suspensions not installed on the haul tuck.
WARNING

Falling parts can cause injury

  • Always use approved lift devices that can safely lift and support component weight.

Step 1

Use an approprieate lift device to position suspension vertically on a bench or fixture. Make sure the suspension is secure from tipping and the bleedes valve (17) faces upward

NOTICE

The fill capacity of each shock absorber is 46 L (12.2 gal).

  • Use only approved oil to fill this component.

Step 2

Fill the suspension with 46 L (12.2 gal) of the approved oil for this application. [Refence : Oil and Lubricant Specifications, page 117].

Step 3

Connect the oil filling device to fitting (21).

CAUTION
  • Trapped air will affect the oil volume and may result in suspension failure.
  • Make sure to release trapped air from inside the suspension.

Step 4

Slowly open the bleeder valve (17) to permit trapped air to escape.

Step 5

Begin to fill the suspension through fitting (21) with 46 L (12.2 gal) of the approved oil for this application. [Reference : Oil and Lubricant Specifications, page 117].

Step 6

Remove the oil filling device from fitting (21) and reinstall the oil fill port cap.

 WARNING

Incorrect handling of gas substances can cause injury.

  • Carefully follow all site gas operating safety procedures, and make sure to put on approved protective clothing and safety goggles when you work with gas substances.

Step 1

Check to make sure isolator valce (15) is correctly installed.

Step 2

Attach the nitrogen charging device to the bleeder valve (17).

Step 3

Througt the bleeder valve (17), charge the suspension with 40 bar (580 psi).

CAUTION

The suspension will extend during charging.

  • Keep clear of the suspension during charging.

Step 4

Read the charge pressure from the nitrogen gauge until the suspension has fully extended to 1170 mm (46.06 in).

Step 5

Close the bleeder valve (17) and let the suspension rest under pressure for 24 hours.

NOTE

After the 24 - hour time period has lapses, the pressure loss cannot exceed 1.5 bar (21 psi).

  • Use an approved lift device that can safely lift and support these weights.

Step 1

Slowly open the air valve.

Step 2

Disconnect the nitrogen charging device.

Step 3

Carefully open the bleeder valve (17) an let the nitrogen escape slowly. Keep a safe distance from any nitrogen that escapes from the suspension during the time the suspension retracts.

Step 4

Once oil begins to flow, immediately close the bleeder valve (17).It is importnt that this procedure is completed without any loss of app-oil The checking dimension of 10 mm (0.79 in) - 25 mm (0.98 in) corresponds to the oil fill volume specifications of 46 L (12.2 gal).

Step 5

Remove the oil filling device from the screw coupling (21)..

NOTE

Store and transport only oil filled suspensions.
